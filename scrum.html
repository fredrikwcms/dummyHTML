Scrum

What is Scrum?

Scrum is a simple framwork that is focus on team collaboration and it helps team build complex products easily. Scrum is not a methodology but it is a science experiment. The product is built in a time serie which is called "sprints". Scrum divides sprint in four stage:

  1. Sprint planning: Team has a plan for the next sprint
  2. Daily stand-up: A quickly meeting for team, 15-minute meeting
  3. Sprint demo: Team tells each other about what they have done in this sprint
  4. Sprint retrospective: Team reviews what is done and what is haven't done and make it better

Scrum has three rolls.

  1. The product owner
  2. Development team
  3. Scrum master

Scrum framework

  1. The product owner creates a product backlog.
  2. During sprints planning, the team picks up the first item from the list and decide how to work on it.
  3. Team has a certain of time.
  4. Scrum master keeps the team focus on its goal.
  5. At the end of sprint, the work should be ready.
  6. The sprint ends with a sprint review.
  7. The circle repeats

Scrum process benifits organization by helping it to incressce the quality of the final product. It copes better with the changes, controlling schedule and time.
